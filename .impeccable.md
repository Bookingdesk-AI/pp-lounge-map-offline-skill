## Design Context

### Users
Internal travel, operations, and data users who need to find, filter, inspect, and compare Priority Pass lounge records quickly across a global airport catalog.

### Brand Personality
Operational, restrained, precise. The interface should feel like an internal enterprise tool: dense, clear, stable, and quiet.

### Aesthetic Direction
Data-dense geospatial console with compact controls, light-neutral surfaces, restrained status color, and no landing-page or marketing presentation. Avoid consumer travel branding, oversized hero typography, decorative ambience, subtitles, intro copy, and helper explanations.

### Design Principles
- Keep only functional text: titles, labels, table/list headers, buttons, statuses, values, and errors.
- Prioritize search, filtering, selection, comparison, and record inspection on desktop and mobile.
- Use dense spacing, clear hierarchy, and strong contrast without decorative cards or promotional language.
- Make mobile a complete bottom-sheet workflow with results, filters, and details available.
- Audit every visible string; remove any copy not required for task completion.
